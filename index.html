No. 04

What Guides Me

1

Autonomy over control

Teams with ownership, clear goals and trust outperform teams that are managed. My job is to remove obstacles, not to add myself as one.

2

Product-minded engineering

Engineers should own the "why." I push teams to validate ideas with prototypes and discovery before committing to a full build.

3

Loosely coupled, independently deployable

Kafka and clean domain boundaries aren't the goal, they're how a team ships on Tuesday without waiting on three others. I architect for independence first, because that's what lets people move fast safely.

4

Still building, still learning

A manager who can't build loses the plot. I came up through PHP and architecture, and I'm deliberately rebuilding that toolkit around AI and Python now, shipping small projects in the open, so my technical opinions stay earned, not remembered.
